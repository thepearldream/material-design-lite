All In Play - Imagination Sessions
An imaginative approach to curriculum and staff development in early childhood classrooms that uses guided play, theater and movement techniques to structure and support children’s active learning.
Our mission is to reach early childhood educators, many of whom are hungry for tools that they can use to better communicate and teach curriculum to their students. All In Play has FJC Fiscal Sponsorship with 501c3 Status.
10 -15 minutes of All In Play™ activities gets children working happily together in groups and captures their attention. It enables me to lead them into other curricular inquiries while sustaining that same spirit of fun. Teachers in my Center have seen what happens to children when I implement All In Play™ and a dozen of them have requested the opportunity to get involved. My children look forward to taking part in All In Play™ scenarios. They have become audience members who can listen quietly as they observe their classmates in action. Organizing the children in space was very interesting. ‘Sticky Feet’ worked. The children became enthusiastic. They enjoy pretending they are glued to the floor! Why isn’t All In Play™ just the standard? I believe it should be..

All In Play Team

Jackie Wildau performed for children as a mime, magician and clown. Her professional work included both ensemble and solo performances in a variety of venues. She used her theater background and these experiences interacting with children to develop a drama enrichment program for preschool children that thrived and grew over 13 years at the Columbia Grammar and Preparatory School. Her work with hundreds of children was the laboratory where the Wildau Method was born. Recognizing the magic that happened in these classes, she decided to share her principles and methods with early childhood teachers. She designed and implemented professional development training utilizing Wildau Method techniques and strategies for the CUNY Research Foundation and Professional Development Institute, NYU Steinhardt School of Education (Brandeis), and New Teacher Academy at Columbia University Teachers College.

Eileen Hall has worked for over 40 years in many different capacities in over more than 40 years in the early childhood field. She taught children from infancy through middle school, directed early childhood and school-age child care centers, founded a child care resource and referral agency and taught teacher preparation college courses. Sharing her experiences and expertise with child care providers, early childhood teachers, parents and college students is the passion that has fueled her professional journey. Weaving child development theory and current research with practical strategies, encouraging reflection and risk taking, and sparking laughter and honest conversation have been the foundation of her workshops, trainings and college classes.
